# Quantum Computing Research Roadmap

This roadmap defines the technical program guiding the notebooks in this repository.  
It structures the work across three integrated pillars:

- **Quantum Algorithms & Optimization**
- **Quantum Molecular Simulation**
- **Quantum Machine Learning for Physical and Biological Systems**

---

## 1. Quantum Algorithms & Optimization

Core computational primitives used across all application domains.

- **Linear Systems**
  - Harrow–Hassidim–Lloyd (HHL)
  - Quantum state preparation and measurement

- **Search and Combinatorics**
  - Grover’s algorithm
  - Constraint satisfaction (e.g., N-Queens)

- **Optimization**
  - QUBO formulations
  - QAOA
  - Unit Commitment and energy-system optimization

---

## 2. Quantum Molecular Simulation

Physics-grounded modeling of chemical and biological systems.

- **Hamiltonian Construction & Mapping**
  - Electronic and effective Hamiltonians  
  - Fermion-to-qubit and continuous-variable encodings  
    (e.g., Jordan–Wigner, Bravyi–Kitaev, CV mappings)

- **Quantum Chemistry**
  - VQE for ground and excited states  
  - Basis-set and ansatz exploration  

- **Reaction and Process Modeling**
  - Redox and electron-transfer systems  
  - Carbon capture and storage  
  - Molecular binding and stability  

---

## 3. Quantum Machine Learning

Hybrid quantum–classical learning for structured, dynamical systems.

- **Feature Encoding & Mapping**
  - Classical-to-quantum encodings  
  - Kernel and reservoir representations  

- **Quantum Reservoir Computing**
  - Qubit-based QRC  
  - Continuous-variable QRC  

- **Time-Series and Dynamics**
  - Electric grid load forecasting  
  - Energy and environmental systems  

- **Biological Learning Tasks**
  - Protein classification  
  - Protein folding  
  - mRNA folding and secondary structure  

---

## 4. Applications

Deployment of the core methods in real domains.

- **Drug Design & Discovery**
  - Protein–ligand binding  
  - Molecular screening  
  - Energy-based ranking of compounds  

- **Energy & Environment**
  - Unit commitment  
  - Carbon capture and storage  

- **Finance**
  - Pricing of call options  
  - Risk and payoff modeling  

---

## 5. Noise and Practicality

- Error modeling and mitigation  
- Practical quantum error correction  
- Hybrid classical–quantum robustness  

---

## Direction

The unifying goal is to build **quantum-enabled computational pipelines** for molecular science, drug discovery, and industrial optimization through the integration of:

- Energy-based molecular modeling  
- Quantum and hybrid optimization  
- Quantum machine learning
